Subject: MULTIVERSE

Every phenomenal object which can be imagined already exists unconditionally and eternally without an instantiation point nor a deletion point from the vantage of reality as an all-encompassing whole (and reality as an all-encompassing whole is aware of every component of itself simultaneously).

If the previous sentence is true, then each noumenal object is unconditionally experienced as a phenomenal object by reality as an all-encompassing whole (which means that reality as an all-encompassing whole experiences indefinitely many phenomenally distinct partial frames of reference simultaneously).

Such a concurrence of multiple partial frames of reference (and that multiple includes each imaginable phenomenal object) could be defined as the one and only possible multiverse throughout all existence (and that multiverse is synonymous with reality as an all-encompassing whole (and reality as an all-encompassing whole is synonymous with nature)).

A partial frame of reference could be defined as exactly one universe (and that universe is subset of the entire multiverse (and that universe is always smaller than the multiverse)).

An omniscient frame of reference could be defined as a field of awareness which encompasses each imaginable universe simultaneously (and that multiverse renders each one of those universes as a static space-time continuum (and each one of those static space-time continuums branches into indefinitely many phenomenally distinct and static space-time continuums within that omniscient field of awareness)).

The word static is synonymous with the perceived and/or imagined absence of change.

The word change is synonymous with the perceived and/or imagined spontaneous instantiation of a particular phenomenon emerging out of pure nothingness.

According to the panpsychic hypothesis, reality as an all-encompassing whole takes place inside of one ubiquitous (and therefore boundless) frame of reference. That ubiquitous frame of reference is fundamentally composed of pure nothingness (and pure nothingness is simultaneous with pure consciousness).

It may be inferred that change, and therefore the passage of time, is only perceptible from a partial frame of reference and not from an omniscient frame of reference (unless omniscience includes the experience of being a partial frame of reference).
