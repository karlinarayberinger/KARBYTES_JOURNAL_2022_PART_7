Earlier today, I wrote some notes down on a piece of paper which I think will end up being used to re-draft the web page on Karlina Object dot WordPress dot Com named TECHNOCRACY. What follows is a more polished version of those notes:

In my idealized society, less than 5% of human beings will comprise the paid workforce (and those humans would be the most competent and educated humans to fill such roles). The other 95% of the workforce would be comprised of robots (and those robots would be orders of magnitude more efficient to employ than humans would be for the same tasks).

What are the vast majority of humans doing if they are not working? Well, each and every human individual is issued a universal minimum income from the government (and people with special needs will be given special vouchers to purchase specific commodities and/or additional money). The vast majority of human beings would spend their time doing what they enjoy. Many would be attending school, doing arts and crafts, participating in sports, and many other voluntary and meaningful pursuits.

How will there be enough money to go around such that everyone gets to live a luxurious lifestyle? Every female-bodied human would be allowed to complete no more than two pregnancies. After a female-bodied human reaches the end of its second pregnancy (whether a live child is yielded from that pregnancy or not), that female-bodied human would be surgically sterilized in order to prevent overpopulation, child neglect, and the propagation of sickly genomes.

In my idealized society, every human being would have unconditional access to the best medical care available (including stem cell tissue replacement and prosthetics). Each human being would be able to extend its lifespan and capabilities indefinitely (which means that the human death rate would be much lower than the human birth rate). Each human being would expect to live for at least 700 years (and if the human population size gets to large, then all human pregnancies will be aborted (even if that means detaining humans and forcing them to go through an abortion and subsequent sterilization procedure)) until vacancies open up due to a sufficient number of humans dying.

What I am interested in elaborating more on is how money would be produced, maintained, and forcibly distributed. Many services would be public utilities such as self driving taxi services, vertical farms which yield more than enough crops to sustain each human individual, public libraries with free Internet access, free gyms, affordable housing, et cetera. Therefore, most of what people would otherwise purchase using money would be free (but there may be daily limits per individual consumer on certain kinds of commodities that are in limited supply).

According to the law of conservation of energy, there is a fixed amount of energy throughout the entire universe. I think the same principle should apply to money. Hence, the economic system I am designing use units of currency whose monetary worth is directly proportional to some number of units of energy used to produce clean energy (especially using photovoltaics) and that clean energy would power the massive Internet where that money “lives” as 100% digital entities. In order to make each unit of money traceable, each unit of money must remain in digital format. It cannot be exchanged for some kind of off-the-grid token such as metal coins (and vice versa).

Where would the money come from? 100% of all money in my idealized society would be produced from solar energy farms (and robots would do the vast majority of the labor involved in converting incoming energy to money and distributing that money to each human being on a monthly, weekly, or even daily basis).

Note: Just as the total amount of entropy in the universe is expected to increase and never decrease as time progresses, the total number of units of money is expected to increase at a rate which is directly proportional to the number of watts of electricity produced from solar farms.
